# Privacy Policy — TagPivot

Last updated: 2026

TagPivot is designed to run entirely on your computer.

We do not collect, transmit, store, or sell your browsing data.

⸻

# What TagPivot accesses

TagPivot reads page content (such as titles and headings) only to generate local tags and summaries inside the overlay.

This processing happens locally in your browser.

No page content is sent anywhere.

⸻

# What data is stored

TagPivot stores a small amount of information locally using Chrome’s extension storage:
	•	Extracted tags
	•	Timestamps
	•	Simple aggregates used to calculate bridges and counterpoints
	•	A short recent history (last 10 captures)

This data:
	•	Is stored only on your device
	•	Is not synced to any server
	•	Is not shared with any third party
	•	Is not used for advertising
	•	Is not sold

⸻

# No accounts, no tracking

TagPivot:
	•	Does not require login
	•	Does not use analytics
	•	Does not use tracking pixels
	•	Does not fingerprint users
	•	Does not transmit browsing history

⸻

# Local access

Because the data is stored locally in your Chrome profile:

Anyone with access to your browser profile on your device may see TagPivot data.

You can clear all stored data at any time by using the Reset button inside the overlay.

⸻

# Permissions

TagPivot requests permission to:
	•	Read page content (to extract tags locally)
	•	Use local storage (to save tag data)

It does not request access to:
	•	Your accounts
	•	Your files
	•	Your system
	•	External servers

⸻

# Changes

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted in this repository.
